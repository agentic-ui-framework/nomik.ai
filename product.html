The Warden

Every workspace ships with a Chief of Staff.

The Warden is a project-aware orchestrator that understands your business inside-out and runs the workspace with you. Anything you can do in the app, it can do in chat.

  • Reads your business: your repo, files, connected accounts, and channel history become a working model of how you operate.
  • Recommends in context: a tailored shortlist of integrations and automations, each with a one-line why, not a generic marketplace.
  • Acts with permission: you grant access; it connects tools, wires triggers, and creates the agents a squad is missing.
  • Keeps watching: it notices the work you keep doing by hand and proposes automating it.

A real org, not a swarm

Intelligence spent where it's worth it.

The org has clear tiers, so the cheapest capable agent does each piece. Spend the brain once on the hard call; let the cheap models execute the rest.

Integrations & triggers

Connect your stack, then automate it.

Turn events into work: a new ticket, payment, or pull request fires a rule, the right squad acts, and risky steps wait for your approval. Build it yourself, or ask the Warden to.

You wire it

Browse the catalog in the app, connect an account (OAuth or API key) into the workspace vault, and build triggers in a visual editor. Gate the risky actions; watch a run log.

The Warden wires it

Ask in plain language, "connect Stripe and alert me when revenue drops." The Warden recommends, sets it up on your grant, names it, gates it, and explains it. Both doors edit the same config.

A new ticket, payment, lead, or PR pings the right squad, which acts and reports back. See the integrations catalog →

Omnichannel & voice

Meet customers wherever they are, even on the phone.

Agents don't just live in your team chat. They talk to your customers on the channels your customers actually use.

Chat & email

Email, web chat, Slack, Teams, drafted or sent within policy, with outbound to customers held for approval.

Messaging

WhatsApp, SMS, Telegram, Instagram DM, answer inbound, run keyword flows, and follow up, all in one inbox.

Voice agents

A voice agent answers and places phone calls, greets the caller, looks things up, resolves or routes to a human. Outbound calls are approval-gated.

The glass box

Nothing your agents do is a black box.

Every agent message carries an inline trace, its thinking, the exact tool calls, and the running cost. You can always look, and you approve the steps that matter.

  • Inline traces: "thought · 3 tools · $0.04" under every message; expand any of it.
  • #events: a real-time, append-only stream of the whole fleet's activity.
  • Decisions ledger: every approval an agent requested and how it resolved.
  • Cost on every turn: rolled up per agent, squad, and workspace.
