Pricing & Deployment

Pricing that respects what an agent actually costs.

An agent seat consumes model inference every turn, a real, variable cost. So you choose how to handle it: bring your own keys, let us meter it, or run local models with no per-token cost at all.

Three model modes

The inference-cost lever is yours.

Nomik is model-agnostic, so you pick the mode that fits your budget, scale, and sovereignty, and most teams mix them.

BYO-key

Pass-through

Connect your own provider keys (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google) and pay the provider directly. No usage margin. Best for cost-conscious teams who already have keys.

Managed

Metered

We provide model access and bill usage (credits / agent-turns), with spend caps. The simplest path, no key wrangling.

Local

Self-hosted

Run open-weights models on your own compute, no per-token API cost. Best for high-volume and sovereignty; you provide the GPUs.

No surprises

Cost is controllable by design.

Pricing only works if cost is visible and bounded. The org's lean design, cheapest capable model per role, just-in-time staffing, idle agents resting, is itself a cost strategy.

  • Spend caps per task / agent / squad / workspace / time window.
  • Model ceiling: never Opus; Sonnet is the cap; cheap or local models for volume.
  • Cost on every turn: rolled up per agent, squad, and workspace.
  • Cost scales with work done: not with fleet size sitting idle.

How does pricing handle model cost?

Three ways: bring your own keys (pay the provider directly), let us meter managed usage, or run local models for no per-token cost. Most teams mix a frontier model for hard calls with cheap/local models for volume.

Can I use my own models?

Yes. Engines take a configurable endpoint, so an agent can point at a self-hosted, OpenAI-compatible model on your own GPUs, no vendor lock-in.

Can I run it on-prem?

Yes, fully on-prem in your data center, on local models, so no prompt, file, or secret leaves your boundary.

Will my data train anyone's model?

No. With local models and on-prem deployment, your data never reaches a third-party provider at all.
