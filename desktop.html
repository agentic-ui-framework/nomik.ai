Nomik Desktop

An AI employee on your machine you can actually trust.

A local-first AI employee that runs on your own machine, with your model and your tools. Safe by default: encrypted secrets, sandbox-first execution, risk-gated tools, a full audit log. And one command puts it on the same roster as the rest of your AI workforce, where every consequential move meets the human approval floor.

The division of labor

Connectors reach out. Nomik Desktop reaches in.

Your Nomik workspace already reaches out to your SaaS: Gmail, Stripe, GitHub, hundreds more. Nomik Desktop is the other half. It reaches in, to your files, your shell, your browser, your local apps. The workspace is the brain and the management layer. Nomik Desktop is the governed hands on your machine.

  • Local-first: your model, your data, your machine. Runs standalone, even fully offline.
  • Workspace-native: one connect and it's an AI employee on the roster, under the same gate, spend caps, and audit trail.
  • One job each: the workspace brain decides and coordinates; Nomik Desktop does the local work, safely.

Safe by default

The defaults are the product.

A local AI employee has the keys to your machine. So Nomik Desktop ships locked down on the first run, not after you read the docs. Everything below is on at install.

Encrypted secrets

Keys live in your OS keychain with an encrypted fallback. The agent process never reads a secret in the clear.

Sandbox-first exec

Shell and file work run sandboxed by default. Reaching the host is an explicit, gated opt-in, never the default.

Risk-gated tools

Every tool declares a risk tier. High-risk actions are default-deny, and the gate runs in the agent loop, not just at the edge.

Loopback-only

It binds to localhost out of the box. LAN or Tailnet access is an explicit opt-in with a printed warning, not a quiet default.

Append-only audit

Every tool call, exec, secret access, and config change lands in a forensic trail you can replay. Nothing happens off the record.

Budgets and caps

Token and spend budgets with per-turn caps. Connected to a workspace, the spend cap and the money floor apply too.

The connect moment

One command, and it joins your workspace.

Run nomik-desktop connect, pick a workspace and a scope, and your local AI employee dials in over the Nomik SDK as a first-class member of the roster. The connect token is stored in your keychain. Nothing else crosses the line.

  • It inherits the glass box: money and destructive actions always wait for a human, with spend caps and RBAC on top.
  • It uses workspace tools: gated connector calls, the same approval floor every AI employee rides. It can never touch a teammate's personal accounts.
  • It lends its hands: your local files, shell, and browser become tools the fleet can use, with your approval, each one risk-classed.
  • It shows its work: it appears in the roster with an External badge, answers @-mentions, and every action is in the activity trail.

Two ways to run it

Great alone. Governed when it counts.

The same binary, both ways. Connecting to a workspace is additive, never required, and it's what turns "reachable" into "governed."

Standalone

Local-only mode

No account needed. A safe personal assistant on your machine: your model, your files and shell and browser, local memory with decay and citations. The safe defaults are on from the first run, so it's strictly locked down out of the box.

Connected

Workspace-connected mode

Run connect and it joins a Nomik workspace as an AI employee. It uses workspace connectors (gated), exposes its local tools to the fleet, and answers in your channels, all under the human approval floor, spend caps, RBAC, and a complete activity trail.

Migration

Coming from OpenClaw? Bring your skills.

Switching should be free. Nomik Desktop imports your existing OpenClaw skills unchanged, then versions and reliability-scores them. Import your config too, and it shows you exactly which defaults it's making safer on the way in.

  • Skills import: the same SKILL.md contract, brought in and scored, not rewritten.
  • Config import: models, channels, and skills mapped over in one pass.
  • Upgrades, shown: secrets move to your keychain, exec goes sandbox-first, the bind goes loopback-only, and you see every change.
Coming from OpenClaw?
# bring your existing skills, unchanged
$ nomik-desktop skills import ~/.openclaw/skills
✓ imported 12 skills · versioned + reliability-scored

# and your config, with the safe upgrades flagged
$ nomik-desktop import-openclaw
✓ secrets  →  moved to your OS keychain
✓ exec     →  sandbox-first
✓ network  →  loopback-only
