Use cases

The AI workforce your business hires.

The pattern is the same in every business: you hire AI employees into each function, connect your stack, and supervise the work in the open, approving only the consequential calls. Here's a day in the life, by business type.

E-commerce · 6 people · ~$4M/yr on Shopify

"Lumio", DTC home goods

Drowning in support tickets, ad busywork, and manual bookkeeping, no time to grow because they're running to stand still.

Departments: Growth · Support · Operations · Nomik

StackKG
  • Overnight: a payment_failed → dunning trigger recovers carts; Support drafts every ticket, sends the routine ones, holds refunds for approval.
  • A call comes in: an AI employee answers by voice, looks up the order, offers store credit, escalates the angry one with a transcript ready.
  • "Plan a spring sale": Growth drafts the campaign and budget; the founder approves the spend and they launch.

What changed: support runs around the clock; the founder spends the day on growth, not the inbox.

SaaS · seed-stage B2B · 12 people

"Cadence", B2B SaaS

Small eng team, a backlog that never shrinks, support and sales falling on the founders.

Departments: Engineering · Product · Growth · Support

StackS
  • A goal in chat: the orchestrator triages, the bug routes to an engineer, the feature goes to a principal for a spec.
  • It ships: the engineer patches and tests green inline; QA verifies; the founder approves the deploy in one click.
  • Signals flow in: triggers turn product feedback into tasks and enterprise leads into ready-to-send outreach.

What changed: the founders set direction and approved a handful of things. Their AI workforce did the rest.

Agency · 15 people · 9 client retainers

"Field & Co", creative + marketing

The bottleneck is coordination, every client wants updates, every project runs in parallel, and senior people drown in status admin.

Workforce: one workspace per client, an account lead (human) + Growth/Content/Design AI employees + a per-workspace Nomik

StackSH
  • Per client: each workspace's Nomik posts a status digest, and AI employees draft posts and creatives the lead approves in threads.
  • A request lands: an inbound email → scope + task trigger turns "can we add a landing page?" into an estimate the lead sends.
  • Month-end: a trigger has timesheets and invoices ready; the owner reviews margins per client.

What changed: more clients, no more coordination overhead. Seniors do senior work; their AI employees do the glue.

Regulated · 40-person EU fintech · on-prem only

"Severn", sovereignty-sensitive

Wants an AI workforce but cannot send customer data, code, or secrets to third-party model providers. Most AI platforms are a non-starter.

Why Nomik fits: deployed on-prem in Severn's own data center, with AI employees on local, self-hosted models, nothing leaves the building.

Stack (in your boundary)P
  • Identical collaboration to the SaaS scenario, departments, board, inline traces, approvals, but every model call hits a local model on Severn's GPUs.
  • A failed-payment → dunning and month-end → reconcile to DATEV run against EU-resident systems under GDPR controls.
  • Compliance reviews the glass-box audit (#events + decisions ledger) to confirm exactly what every AI employee did.

What changed: a regulated business gets the full leverage of an AI workforce without a data-exit risk, the capability competitors can't offer.

The common thread

Every business runs the same loop.

Intent in, work out, with humans on the calls that matter.

Intent in, work out

You state a goal in plain language; the org decomposes and staffs itself.

Triggers do the recurring work

Dunning, tickets, reconciliation, alerts, they run automatically and show up as visible, approvable work.

Humans own judgment

You approve spend, deploys, and customer messages, and do the creative and relationship work.

One timeline

It all happens in shared channels with full traces, nothing is a black box.
