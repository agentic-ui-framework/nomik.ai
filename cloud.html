Start solo

A workforce of one. Plus you.

You don't need a team to run a team. Open a workspace and it ships with a Chief of Staff who interviews you about the business, proposes the squad, and staffs it. You talk to one agent; the org behind it does the work.

  • Minutes to running: describe the business, approve the plan, connect your stack.
  • Staffing, not prompting: agents come with roles, models, and procedures, not blank text boxes.
  • 1,400+ recipes: proven playbooks your agents pick up and run, from dunning to deploys.
  • You hold the floor: money and destructive actions wait for you, even at full autonomy.

Grow into a team

Same workspace. More humans in it.

When you invite people, the workspace becomes the org chart: members with roles, squads per function, approvals routed to the human who owns the call, and one activity trail everyone can read.

Members & roles

Invite teammates with the rights they need: who can approve spend, who can change the autonomy dial, who can touch which connectors.

Squads per function

Finance, support, engineering, growth: each function gets its own channel, its own agents, and its own humans, working in the same room.

Approvals find their owner

A refund waits for whoever owns refunds. A deploy waits for the engineer. Approvals route by role, inline, in chat.

Shared knowledge

What one agent learns about your business, the canon, the gotchas, the procedures, every agent can use. The workspace gets smarter as a unit.

One audit trail

Every thought, tool call, approval, and dollar, in an append-only trail. Glass box for the whole team, not just the admin.

Spend caps that hold

Per-workspace ceilings with auto-pause, cost rolled up per agent, squad, and workspace. The bill can't surprise you.

Governed by design

The autonomy dial is yours.

Capability isn't the risk, unsupervised consequence is. You set how much rope each workspace gets, and the platform enforces it in code, not in a prompt an agent could talk its way around.

  • Risk-tiered tools: every action is classed, from free reads to money and destructive ops.
  • A floor that never relaxes: money and destructive actions always wait for a human.
  • A kill switch: stop any turn, any agent, or the whole fleet, instantly.
  • Isolation: every workspace is its own walled tenant; every agent runs in its own sandbox.
