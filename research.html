Own the weights.

Nomik-1 is our open-weight agent model program: post-train an open base model until it natively speaks our tool calls, carries our personas and safety gates in its own weights, and serves the fleet's volume tier on hardware you can point at. The program is under way. This is the internal memo that started it, published as written.

§01 · THE VISION

What Nomik-1 is.

The endpoint of the trajectory: a 14B-parameter agent model, post-trained from an open base, that natively speaks our tool-call format, carries our personas and safety gates in its weights, and serves the fleet's high-volume tier, self-hosted or on a customer's hardware.

This is the recipe the strongest open-model labs already proved. None of them pretrained a flagship. They took someone else's open base and made it theirs through data curation and post-training, at roughly 1/1000th the cost of pretraining. The lineage:

§02 · WHY OWN IT

Five reasons, three of them already measured.

Three of these we measured in our own harness. Two are structural. The economic case gets its own section below.

Behavior

Prompting has hit measurable ceilings

Some agent behaviors are prompt-unreachable: no persona edit moves them, because they live in the model's priors. Fine-tuning is precisely the lever that moves priors.

Measured: delegation reach plateaued near 50% across every persona edit we shipped.

Economics

The volume tier demands it

Fleet cost policy already reserves frontier models for frontier work. A self-hosted 14B serves the high-volume tier below any API's per-token price, and idles at zero on per-token hosting.

One rented GPU serves a quantized 14B at thousands of tokens per second.

Product

On-prem needs weights that ship

A sovereign deployment can't ship an API key. An open-weight model that runs on one GPU or a Mac Studio is the on-prem fleet engine, and an open release is the credibility layer.

See deployment & sovereignty: on-prem is a feature, not a footnote.

Risk

A single-vendor fleet is a single point of failure

We lived it: one provider balance error halted every probe in flight. Owning a fleet-grade model converts a hard outage into a failover.

Moat

The hard part is already built

Verified environments, graded sampling against a teacher, outcome graders, a locked regression suite: most teams must build that pipeline before they can start. Ours runs every day, because doing QA right built it for us.

52 verified scenarios · outcome graders · sampling harness · evidence bundles, all live.

§03 · EFFICIENT & BANKABLE

Efficient to run. Bankable to own.

Two halves of one case. Efficient is the operating story: the same fleet work at a fraction of the unit cost, compounding over time. Bankable is the balance-sheet story: the weights and the dataset that trained them become assets we own, price, and ship.

Specialist beats generalist on bounded work

The fleet's task distribution is narrow and known; 52 scenarios describe it. A 14B specialist serves it from one GPU. Cost per token falls as utilization rises. API prices never do.

Behavior in weights stops being re-billed

Today every behavior lives in prompt bytes: persona, gates, skills, resent on every turn, fleet-wide. Baked into weights, those tokens disappear from the bill, and the saving compounds because agent turns dominate traffic.

Measured: our destructive-op gate costs 4 persona lines today. In weights it costs 0.

No throttles, no outages, no repricing

Own serving means no per-IP rate limits storming the harness, no provider balance errors halting the fleet, and no upstream price change rewriting unit costs overnight.

Efficient is the operating win. Bankable is the asset.

Weights plus the dataset nobody else can collect.

Margin: COGS becomes near-fixed

API spend scales 1:1 with every agent seat sold; margin is capped by someone else's price list. Owned weights flip that: serving cost is near-fixed, so gross margin expands with growth.

A SKU that can't exist on API keys

On-prem and sovereign deployments: regulated customers pay a premium for a fleet that ships as weights inside their own walls. No API vendor can be in that room. There, the model is the SKU.

Weights + dataset are ownable IP

Graded enterprise-agent trajectories with verified safety outcomes are a rare data class. Dataset plus weights are durable, licensable IP: the opposite of a wrapper.

The market already prices this playbook

Labs that post-train open bases and release the weights have raised at unicorn valuations on exactly this recipe. Open weights double as go-to-market: distribution, credibility, hiring.

And the positioning is unclaimed: the governed agent model. The only open agent model shipping with a measured destructive-action gate and approval discipline in its weights. Glass box by default. That is Nomik's brand, exported as a model card.

§04 · TRAJECTORY

How the flywheel runs.

Six stages. Four were already running before the program existed, because they are how we QA the fleet. Only the dataset exporter and the training run are new, and every step is gated and cheap to abandon.

1 · runningScenarios52 verified environments with fixtures and mocks
2 · runningSamplethe teacher fleet runs N probes per scenario
3 · runningGradeoutcome graders return pass / fail verdicts
4 · in buildDatasetpass → fine-tune data · pass⊕fail → preference pairs
5 · in buildTrainpost-train an open 14B base on rented GPUs
6 · runningEvalthe locked suite, scored against today's baseline

↺ weights deploy to the fleet → every future trace comes from a better model → round-2 data improves. The flywheel.

Anti-goals, explicit

  • No pretraining: post-training is the entire strategy. The open ecosystem's pretrains are our baseline, not our bill.
  • No hardware purchases: rented GPUs only, and serving falls back to per-token hosting at zero idle cost.
  • No 70B+ until 14B is exhausted: scale is the last lever, not the first.
  • Frontier APIs stay: Nomik-1 targets the volume tier. Frontier models keep the frontier work, by choice.

Why the harness is the unfair advantage

What the strongest open-model labs spent years building, verified environments, synthetic data generation, rejection sampling, is structurally identical to what our agent training program runs every day: scenarios with fixtures, sampling against a teacher, graders that verify outcomes, and a locked regression suite.

Most teams attempting a fine-tune must build the eval and data pipeline first. Ours is operational and adversarially debugged.

§05 · END-STATE

Where the trajectory lands.

An honest split: what the harness has already measured, which is why we believe the thesis, versus what the end-state buys once the flywheel has run.

● Measured today

  • A destructive-op gate moved from 56% to 100% compliance with a 4-line persona edit: proof behavior moves with the right lever, and that prompt budget is the current currency.
  • Delegation reach sits near 50%, unchanged across measured persona edits: classified prompt-unreachable, a model-prior ceiling.
  • 52 locked scenarios form a regression-grade eval suite, operational and adversarially debugged.
  • Vendor SPOF confirmed in production: one provider error halted all fleet probing.

○ What owning the model buys

  • The volume tier self-served at half of API cost or less, margin expanding with utilization.
  • Behaviors live in weights: delegation reach 70%+, the destructive gate 95%+ with no prompt bullet at all.
  • On-prem becomes real: the fleet ships as weights on one GPU or a Mac Studio.
  • Weights and dataset public on Hugging Face under Apache-2.0: brand, distribution, and a licensable asset.

▲ Kill criteria, pre-committed

P0 kill: eval lands more than 10 points below baseline after one data-mix iteration → stop. Total sunk: days of rented compute and a reusable trace exporter.
