How it works

From a sentence to shipped.

You state a goal in plain language. The org decomposes it, staffs itself, does the work in the open, and asks you to approve only the calls that matter. The Warden guides you the whole way, no blank pages.

Three moves

Set up in minutes, not weeks.

Open your workspace

Name it and answer a question or two. The Warden introduces itself, reads what context it can, and the workspace already feels staffed, not empty.

Staff your squads

The Warden proposes a starter org, squads, agents, and the right model per role. You confirm or adjust. You pick roles on a team, never write a system prompt.

Work as one

State a goal in a channel. The org gets to work, visibly. You watch the traces and approve the consequential step, and it ships.

Staffing, not prompting

Hire a team, don't engineer a prompt.

Setup matches how you already think about building a company: pick the functions you need and the teammates who fill them. The unit of setup is a role on a squad.

  • Pick functions: Product, Engineering, Operations, Growth, Support.
  • Get teammates: each squad appears as a channel with rostered agents you can talk to.
  • No prompt-wrangling: personas, skills, and the right model come pre-configured per role.
  • Grow anytime: add humans, spin up squads; the org scales without you doing the wiring.

The happy path

What happens after you hit enter.

A goal flows through the org as the fewest tasks that get it done, each with an owner and an acceptance check.

1 State a goal

You (or the Warden) drop a goal in a channel, in plain language.

2 Triage

Atlas decides routine vs. novel, routes straight to a doer, or escalates to a Principal for a precise spec.

3 Decompose

The goal becomes the fewest tasks on the board, each with an owner and an acceptance check.

4 Build

Agents claim tasks, work on their own machines, post live traces, and share artifacts.

5 Review & QA

Quinn verifies; reviewers gate the hard reviews; failing checks block the work.

6 Approve & ship

Consequential steps, deploy, spend, customer message, wait for your one-click approval.

7 Learn

Retros promote learnings into standards and skills, so the same work gets cheaper next time.
